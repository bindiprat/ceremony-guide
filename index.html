Bindi & Pratik's Wedding Ceremony

Baraat

The groom arrives at the wedding venue accompanied by family and friends, dancing to music as a joyful procession.

Jai Mala

The bride and groom exchange floral garlands in a ritual that symbolizes mutual acceptance and the beginning of their union.

Ganesh Puja

Prayers are offered to Lord Ganesha to remove all obstacles and to bless the ceremony with prosperity and peace.

Vaar Puja

The bride’s parents welcome the groom with a tilak on his forehead and perform an aarti to honor him as part of their family.

Kanya Aagman

The bride enters the mandap with grace, traditionally veiled behind an antarpat—a silk cloth held by her brothers. When the antarpat is lowered, it signifies the beginning of their union.

Kanyadaan & Hasta Milap

The bride’s parents place her hands into the groom’s as an act of giving her away. The ritual of tying the bride’s scarf with the groom’s scarf represents their physical and spiritual union.

Agni Puja & Mangal Pheras

The sacred fire is lit as a witness to the marriage. The couple circles the fire four times, each phera symbolizing a fundamental pillar of life:

  • Dharma: Righteousness and moral values
  • Artha: Prosperity and responsibilities
  • Kama: Emotional and spiritual fulfillment
  • Moksha: Liberation and spiritual growth

Traditionally, during the final phera, the bride’s brother may gently tug at the groom’s toe as a light-hearted gesture—an affectionate rite that acknowledges the shift in familial bonds.

Saptapadi

With each of the seven sacred steps, the couple makes one vow to one another:

  1. To provide nourishment and a healthy life
  2. To grow in strength and courage
  3. To prosper together through work and wealth
  4. To share love and mutual respect
  5. To raise strong and virtuous children
  6. To care for each other through sickness and health
  7. To remain lifelong companions with loyalty and understanding

After completing the seven steps, the one who sits down first is believed to hold a guiding role in the household—an age-old tradition filled with symbolic grace.

Mangalsutra & Sindoor

The groom adorns the bride with sindoor (vermilion) in the parting of her hair and ties the mangalsutra around her neck, signifying eternal love, respect, and marital commitment.

Kansar Bhojan

The bride and groom share a sweet , their first meal together as a couple.

Akhand Saubhagyavati

Married women from the bride’s family bless her with whispered words of fortune and everlasting marital bliss.

Aashirwad & Vidai

The couple seeks blessings from elders before the bride departs to begin her new life. The vidai marks a deeply emotional farewell from her parental home.
